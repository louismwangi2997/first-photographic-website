MY MOST MEMORABLE AND FAVOURITE PHOTOS

Below is a list of some of my favourate photographs.This includes:

  • Snowy mountain peaks in the himalayas -The Himalayas are the highest mountain range in the world, stretching across several countries including India, Nepal, Bhutan, China, and Pakistan. Formed millions of years ago by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, they are home to the world’s tallest peak, Mount Everest. The Himalayas play a crucial role in shaping the climate of Asia and are the source of many major rivers. Rich in biodiversity and cultural significance, they are also home to diverse communities and have long been a place of exploration, spirituality, and natural beauty.

  • City lights of New York -is one of the most famous and influential cities in the world. Founded in the early 17th century by Dutch settlers as New Amsterdam, it later came under British control and was renamed New York. Located on the east coast of the United States, the city grew into a global center for finance, culture, media, and immigration. Known for landmarks like Statue of Liberty and Times Square, New York City is often called “the city that never sleeps” due to its vibrant and fast-paced lifestyle.

  • Sunset ViewsThe beauty of a sunset lies in its soft, glowing colors that paint the sky with warmth and calm, creating a peaceful moment as day gently fades into night. It brings a sense of reflection, hope, and quiet wonder, reminding us to pause and appreciate the simple, fleeting beauty of nature. Each sunset is unique, blending shades of gold, orange, pink, and purple, creating a breathtaking scene that never repeats the same way twice.

  • Historical MonumentsThe Colosseum is a historic landmark located in Rome, Italy. Built in the 1st century AD during the reign of the Roman Empire, it was used for public spectacles such as gladiator fights and large entertainment events. As one of the greatest architectural achievements of ancient times, the Colosseum could hold tens of thousands of spectators. Today, it stands as a powerful symbol of Roman engineering, history, and culture.
